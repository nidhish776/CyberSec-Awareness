The Problem

Why Cybersecurity Matters

Every day, millions of people are targeted by hackers, scammers, and fraudsters. No one is safe — especially the young and the elderly.

💀
Account Hacking
Young people's Instagram, WhatsApp, and Snapchat accounts are hijacked by hackers using phishing, credential stuffing, and brute force attacks. Private photos and messages are then used for blackmail.
🪪
Identity Theft
Personal data like Aadhaar, PAN, and bank details are stolen through fake forms and apps. Criminals use this to apply for loans, open accounts, or commit fraud in your name.
📞
Online Scams & OTP Fraud
Older individuals are daily targets of fake bank calls, UPI scams, and lottery frauds. Urgency tactics and impersonation trick victims into sharing OTPs and bank credentials.

    Simulation

    Phishing Attack Simulator

    Can you spot a fake email? Analyze the message below and decide if it's legitimate or a phishing attempt.

    From: security@sbi-alerts.net
    Subject: ⚠️ URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!

    Dear Valued Customer,

    We have detect suspicious activity on you account. Your Internet Banking access will be permanently blocked unless you verify your details immediately.

    Click the button below to verify your account within 24 hours or all transactions will be suspended.

    Verify Now — Click Here
    ✅ Correct! This is a phishing email. Here's why:
    🌐 Fake domain: "sbi-alerts.net" — real SBI uses "onlinesbi.com"
    ⏰ Urgency tactic: "24 hours" creates panic to stop you thinking
    ✍️ Grammar error: "detect suspicious activity on you account"
    👤 Generic greeting: "Valued Customer" — banks use your real name
    🔗 Suspicious link: Hover to see it goes to a fake domain
    🎣
    How to Spot Phishing
    🔍 Check the sender's email domain carefully
    ⏰ Beware of urgent or threatening language
    ✍️ Look for spelling and grammar mistakes
    🔗 Hover over links before clicking
    🏦 Banks never ask OTPs over email/phone
    📞 Call the company directly if unsure
    🛡️
    If You Clicked a Phish Link
    1. Don't enter any details
    2. Close the tab immediately
    3. Change your passwords
    4. Report to cybercrime.gov.in
    5. Alert your bank if financial

    Fraud Awareness

    Common Scams & Red Flags

    Know the tricks cybercriminals use so you never fall victim.

    Financial Fraud
    OTP & UPI Scam
    • Caller claims to be from your bank
    • Asks you to "verify" by sharing OTP
    • Says your account will be blocked
    • Sends UPI collect request posing as refund
    🛑 Real banks NEVER ask for OTP
    Employment Fraud
    Fake Job Offer Scam
    • Too-good-to-be-true salary offer
    • No interview or easy online test only
    • Asks for registration fee upfront
    • Sent via WhatsApp from unknown number
    🛑 Legit jobs never charge fees
    Tech Support Fraud
    Fake IT Support Scam
    • Pop-up says your device is hacked
    • Gives a number to call "Microsoft" / "Apple"
    • Asks for remote access to your device
    • Claims you owe money for "virus removal"
    🛑 Never give remote access to strangers
    URL Safety

    Website Safety Checker

    Check if a website uses secure HTTPS. Never enter passwords or payment info on HTTP sites.

    What the Padlock Icon Means
    🔒 HTTPS — Encrypted connection. Your data is protected in transit.
    🔓 HTTP — Unencrypted. Hackers can intercept your data (passwords, card numbers) using man-in-the-middle attacks.
    🔍
    URL Red Flags
    ❌ http:// instead of https://
    ❌ Misspelled brand names (amaz0n.com)
    ❌ Extra words (sbi-bank-verify.com)
    ❌ Suspicious TLDs (.xyz, .tk, .ml)
    ❌ Numbers replacing letters (g00gle.com)
    🔒
    SSL Certificate
    SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) encrypts all data between your browser and the server. Always check for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar before entering sensitive information.
    Education

    Learning Hub

    Simple, actionable cybersecurity knowledge everyone should know.

    ✅ Do This
    • ✓ Use strong, unique passwords for each account
    • ✓ Enable 2FA on all important accounts
    • ✓ Keep your software and apps updated
    • ✓ Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi
    • ✓ Verify before clicking links in SMS/email
    • ✓ Check app permissions before installing
    • ✓ Back up your data regularly
    ❌ Don't Do This
    • ✗ Share your OTP with anyone — ever
    • ✗ Reuse the same password across sites
    • ✗ Click links from unknown numbers
    • ✗ Use public Wi-Fi for banking
    • ✗ Accept unknown friend requests blindly
    • ✗ Download apps from unofficial sources
    • ✗ Share personal info in WhatsApp groups
    📸
    Instagram Safety
    Set your account to private. Don't accept requests from strangers. Never share your location publicly. Beware of fake brand giveaway DMs asking for your details.
    💬
    WhatsApp Scams
    Ignore messages promising prizes or jobs. Never scan unknown QR codes. Enable two-step verification. Be cautious of "family member in trouble" calls asking for money transfers.
    🔐
    Two-Factor Authentication
    Enable 2FA on Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot access your account without the second verification step.
